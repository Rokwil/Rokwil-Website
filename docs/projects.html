Keystone Park

Hammarsdale–Cato Ridge, N3 Corridor ±152 Hectares N3 Direct Access Mega DC Logistics Precinct

Keystone Park is a ±152-hectare serviced industrial logistics precinct strategically positioned on the N3 corridor between Durban Port and the interior. Located at Hammarsdale–Cato Ridge with direct N3 access and visibility, the node is profiled by municipal and industry sources as a catalytic logistics precinct that has attracted blue-chip occupiers.

The development offers fully serviced platforms suited to footprints from ~3,776 m² up to ~55,051 m², with some projects exceeding 100,000 m² under roof. Road geometry and yards are configured for high-throughput warehousing operations. The precinct's value proposition is tied to the N3 upgrade program and planned inland port activity in Cato Ridge, which shortens long-haul trucking times and reduces congestion closer to the Port.

Keystone Park reached maturity in 2020 with large platforms sold and build-to-suit projects underway. The development has created thousands of permanent jobs—including approximately 800 at Dromex alone—and positioned KwaZulu-Natal as a key economic gateway linking Durban port to the interior via the N3. The development has also seen third-party investment, including Growthpoint's acquisition of a 13,870 m² logistics asset for R136 million, validating the node's investment appeal.

Available Platforms: Currently 7 platforms remaining, ranging from 13,000 m² to 54,335 m², offering opportunities for new occupiers to join this established logistics hub.

Anchor Occupiers & Facilities

  • Mr Price Group - Highly mechanised National Distribution Centre, 100,000 m² under roof. Smart-enabled warehouse engineered for climate resilience by Arup. Features flood-resilient design with integrated monitoring systems.
  • Ackermans - Mega Distribution Centre, ~97,000 m² facility, with plans for an even bigger facility. One of the early anchor tenants alongside Mr Price.
  • Pepkor / PEP - National Distribution Centre, ~110,000–125,000 m² fully automated mega-facility. Recognised in 2023 specialist awards for ground-improvement works. Features advanced sustainability measures including rainwater harvesting and on-site storage.
  • Dromex - Large safety equipment manufacturer employing approximately 800 people in the precinct.
  • Kerry Ingredients - Sub-Saharan blending plant, significant investment in the precinct.
  • Nu Dawn Eggs - Egg distribution depot, nearing completion.
  • Malda Pack - Early building constructed in the precinct.
  • Growthpoint Properties - Acquired 13,870 m² logistics asset in Keystone for R136 million, demonstrating third-party investor confidence in the node.

Sustainability & Infrastructure Resilience

Keystone Park and its tenants have implemented comprehensive resilience measures to address operational challenges. The precinct features advanced water management systems including rainwater harvesting, tanker bridging, and on-site storage capacity to mitigate service intermittency. These measures ensure operational continuity and demonstrate Rokwil's commitment to sustainable, resilient infrastructure development.
